Our Best Books On Show:

Book 1

Behind the Mask: My Autobiography is an autobiography by Tyson Fury. It was published by Penguin Random House imprint Century on 14 November 2019. It portrays the narrative of how Fury defeated the long-reigning unified heavyweight world champion Wladimir Klitschko in 2015, his battles with mental health following the win and his subsequent recovery process.

Book 2

In My Story, legendary Liverpool and England captain Steven Gerrard reminisces the successes and failures of a twenty-year career at the pinnacle of English and global football. Steven Gerrard is an inspiration to fans and footballers alike as the only player to have scored in an FA Cup final, a League Cup final, a UEFA Cup final, and a Champions League final. He spent the following 28 years and over 700 games devoted to his beloved Liverpool after joining at the age of eight. His loyalty assures that he will be remembered not simply as one of Anfield's all-time greats, but also as one of England's finest footballers.

Book 3

In the ultimate insider's account of the most nerve-wracking yet enthralling three-and-a-half months in English cricket history, Ben Stokes tells the story of England's electrifying first-ever Cricket World Cup success, as well as this summer's momentous Ashes Test series. Was England's last man standing in both innings in the World Cup final and in the famous Headingley Test Match.

Book 4

Ian Wright, Arsenal legend, England striker and TV pundit extraordinaire, is one of modern football's most fascinating and pertinent individuals. Everyone's desire to see him to rise from a South London council estate to national treasure. From 'boring, boring Arsenal' to inside the Wenger Revolution; from a week in prison to inspiring young offenders, Ian will reveal everything about his extraordinary life and career. Wrighty will also speculate candidly on how retirement affects athletes, why George Graham deserves a statue, social media, music, breaking Arsenal's goal-scoring record, racism, the pure joy of playing with Dennis Bergkamp and of course, what he thinks of Spurs.
